A study in restraint — on starting a journal.

May 2, 2026 Tangerang 4 min read

For years I’ve treated writing the way I treat photography — an activity I respect from a distance, occasionally rehearse, and almost never publish. The drafts pile up in private notebooks, in the margins of design docs, in Slack messages I never send. So this is the first attempt at a different posture: writing, in public, on a slow but steady cadence.

The plan is modest. I want to write down the things I keep explaining out loud at work — why a database CI/CD platform is more about culture than tooling; why ECS Fargate Spot is best treated as a probabilistic resource; why a query-aware proxy is the cheapest way to make a legacy database safe again. If even one of these notes saves another engineer a week of confusion, the project earns its keep.

What you’ll find here

Three rooms in the house, plus this one:

  • About — who I am, what I’m working on, where to find me.
  • CV — the formal record. Nothing surprising.
  • Projects — what I’ve built that’s worth pointing at.
  • Writings — this room. Essays and notes, posted as I finish them.

On the design

I rebuilt this site twice. The first attempt leaned into a heavy, ornamental aesthetic — serif everything, dark walnut palette, gold accents. It looked like a tribute act to a 1980s scholarly journal, which is not really what I wanted to be reading in 2026.

What you’re looking at now is the second pass: light, quiet, mostly typography and whitespace. Apple’s editorial pages were the closest reference — the same restraint with display type, the same trust that good spacing carries more meaning than most decorations. The body text is set in Newsreader, a serif tuned for screens, because this is meant to be a place where prose is the work.

The point of writing slowly, in public, is to discover what you actually believe.

What’s next

I have two essays in flight: one on lessons from running schema-as-code across 250+ databases, and one on the design of Lens — the query-aware proxy I’m currently building. I’ll publish them when they’re less embarrassing than they are right now.

Until then, thanks for reading. If you’d like to get in touch, drop me an email.
